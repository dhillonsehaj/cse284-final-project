================================================================================
  3. CROSS-METHOD COMPARISON
================================================================================

Both methods identify NA20320–NA20321 as the most related pair:
  KING kinship = 0.2446 (expected ~0.25 for 1st degree)
  PI_HAT       = 0.5049 (expected ~0.5 for 1st degree)

KING is more conservative: 323 related pairs (> 0.0442) vs --genome's
29,465 pairs at PI_HAT >= 0.125. The inflated count in --genome reflects
population structure bias in the method-of-moments estimator, which
KING's robust estimator handles better.

KING provides a single kinship coefficient; --genome additionally gives
Z0/Z1/Z2 probabilities, enabling relationship type classification
(e.g., distinguishing parent-child from full siblings).
